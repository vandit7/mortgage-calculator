How Extra Payments Work on Your Mortgage

When you make extra payments on your mortgage, those funds are typically applied directly to the principal balance of your loan. This reduces the amount of money that earns interest, which can save you significant amounts over the life of the loan.

⚠️ Important: Always specify "Principal Only" when making extra payments to ensure your lender doesn't just treat it as a "pre-payment" of next month's interest.

Extra payments can be made in various ways: increasing your regular monthly payment, making bi-weekly payments, or applying lump sums from bonuses. Our calculator helps you model these scenarios to find the approach that best fits your financial situation.

Extra Payment FAQ

Will my lender charge a penalty for extra payments?

In the USA, most conventional mortgages do NOT have prepayment penalties. However, some subprime or older loans might. Always confirm "No Prepayment Penalty" with your lender before starting.

Should I pay off my mortgage or high-interest credit cards first?

Generally, you should pay off credit cards first as they often have rates above 20%. Once high-interest debt is gone, apply those savings to your mortgage principal.

What is a "Mortgage Recast"?

A recast is when you make a large lump sum payment and the lender recalculates your monthly payment to be lower. This is different from a simple extra payment which keeps the payment the same but ends the loan faster.
