Understanding Your Mortgage Payment (PITI)

Your monthly mortgage payment is more than just paying back the money you borrowed. Most homeowners pay what is called PITI, which stands for:

  • Principal: The portion of your payment that actively reduces the loan balance.
  • Interest: The fee charged by the lender for borrowing the money.
  • Taxes: Real estate property taxes required by your local government.
  • Insurance: Homeowners insurance to protect against damage and liability.
🔍 What about PMI? If you put down less than 20%, you'll likely pay Private Mortgage Insurance. Our calculator automatically estimates this at ~0.5% of your loan amount per year.

Mortgage Calculator FAQ

How do I calculate my mortgage manually?

The formula is M = P [ i(1 + i)^n ] / [ (1 + i)^n – 1 ], where P is principal, i is monthly interest rate, and n is number of months. Or, just use our free tool!

What is a good down payment?

While 20% is the gold standard (to avoid PMI), many first-time buyers use FHA loans with as little as 3.5% down or conventional loans with 3-5% down.

Does the calculator include HOA fees?

Currently, this tool focuses on PITI. If you have HOA fees, you should add them to the "New Total" shown in the results for a complete picture.
