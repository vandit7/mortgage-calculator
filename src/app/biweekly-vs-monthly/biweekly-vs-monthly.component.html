Biweekly vs Monthly Mortgage Payments: Which Saves More?

Discover how switching from monthly to biweekly payments can save you $40,000+ in interest and cut years off your mortgage.

Understanding Biweekly vs Monthly Payments

Most homeowners make 12 monthly mortgage payments each year. With biweekly payments, you make 26 payments of half your monthly amount (spread across 2 weeks instead of monthly). This seemingly small change results in one extra full payment per year, which accelerates payoff and significantly reduces total interest paid over time.

For a $300,000 mortgage at 6.5%, switching to biweekly can save approximately $38,962 in interest and shave 3 years off your loan—all without dramatically increasing your monthly cash flow since biweekly amounts align naturally with twice-monthly paychecks.

How Biweekly Payments Work

The Math Behind Biweekly Savings

Monthly Schedule:

  • Pay $1,896.20 × 12 months = $22,754.40 per year
  • 26 biweekly periods per year

Biweekly Schedule:

  • Pay $948.10 × 26 payments = $24,650.60 per year
  • This equals 13 full monthly payments instead of 12!
  • Extra payment per year: approximately $1,896.20

Result: One extra full payment annually, compounded over 30 years = significant interest reduction

Pros and Cons of Biweekly Payments

✅ Advantages

  • Automatic Extra Payment: Get one extra payment per year without conscious effort
  • Paycheck Alignment: Many employers pay biweekly, making payments natural to your cash flow
  • Significant Savings: Save $40,000+ in interest over 30 years on a standard mortgage
  • Accelerated Payoff: Shave 3-5 years off typical 30-year loan
  • Less Temptation: Smaller amounts per payment feel more manageable

⚠️ Disadvantages

  • Setup Requirements: Must coordinate with lender or third party to set up
  • Potential Fees: Some lenders charge $20-50 annual fees for biweekly processing
  • Discipline Needed: Banks may hold 26th payment; must ensure it's applied to principal
  • Not Always Optimal: If earning high investment returns, extra payments may not be best use of cash
  • Lender Variation: Not all lenders support biweekly; some have restrictions

How to Set Up Biweekly Payments

1

Contact Your Lender

Call your servicer to ask if they support biweekly payments. Ask about any fees, setup requirements, and how they handle the extra 26th payment. Some lenders hold it; others apply immediately.

2

Understand Their Process

Clarify how biweekly payments are processed. Will they convert to monthly for their system? Will the 26th payment reduce principal? Get details in writing to avoid payment misapplication.

3

Set Up Automatic Withdrawals (Optional)

Many lenders allow automated ACH (bank account) biweekly withdrawals, which ensures consistency. This is ideal if your paycheck is biweekly.

4

Monitor Your Account

After your first few payments, verify that they're being properly applied. Your loan statement should show principal declining faster than a standard monthly schedule.

5

Verify Principal Application

Ensure the extra payment is reducing your principal balance, not being held in escrow or applied to future interest. If misapplied, contact your lender immediately.
