When Should You Refinance?

Refinancing generally makes sense when you can lower your interest rate by at least 0.75% to 1%. This "rule of thumb" helps ensure that the monthly savings are significant enough to justify the closing costs, which typically range from 2% to 5% of the loan amount.

💡 Pro Tip: If you plan to stay in your home for more than 5 years, even a 0.5% rate drop can result in massive long-term savings.

The "Break-Even" Calculation

Your break-even point is the number of months it takes for your monthly savings to cover the cost of the refinance. For example, if your closing costs are $5,000 and you save $200 per month, your break-even point is 25 months.

Refinance FAQ

How much does it cost to refinance?

Expect to pay between 2% and 5% of your total loan balance in closing costs, which includes appraisal fees, title insurance, and origination fees.

Does refinancing hurt my credit score?

A refinance involves a "hard inquiry," which may cause a temporary dip of a few points. However, consistently making payments on a more affordable loan will help your score in the long run.

Can I refinance with bad credit?

Yes, though you may not get the best rates. Some government-backed programs like FHA Streamline Refinance have more flexible credit requirements.
